Hilsha

Hilsa is most well known as a Bengali’s darling in cuisine but there is more to the fish than the taste. Known as Ilish/Hilsa herring,it gives livelihood to more than 5 million people in Bengal alone. Hilsa was called "Matsyaraja" – the king of all fishes. It was said Illisah jitapiyusah (Hilsa surpasses nectar).

  • Scientific Name: Tenualosa ilisha
  • Average Length: Varies, 40cm to 60cm
  • Average Lifespan: 4 years
  • Habitat: Bengal delta and streams

Physical Profile of Hilsa Fish: The body of Hilsa is very dense and fairly deep with dorsal and ventral profile. It has uniform medium-sized scales that gives the skin a metallic silver shine. A large-sized hilsa weighs about 2.5 kg. Female Hilsas are usually larger than males. The Hilsa is known to be a fast swimmer and can cover even 60 kilometers at a time. It attains maturity within 1 to 2 years.

Hilsa as Food: Hilsa is an oily fish and rich in essential omega 3 fatty acids. A recent experiment has shown it has beneficial effects in decreasing cholesterol and insulin level. Bengali fish curry is a popular dish made with mustard oil or seed. The Bengalis popularly call this dish shorshe ilish. Hilsha due to its popularity is exported worldwide.
